7)7 Foundational Principles of Privacy by Design

The universal principles of the Fair Information Practices (FIPs) are affirmed and extended by Privacy by Design, which seeks the highest global standard possible. The objectives of PbD – ensuring privacy and gaining personal control over one's information and, for organizations, gaining sustainable competitive advantages – are accomplished by applying the following Principles:

•Proactive not Reactive; Preventative not Remedial
•Privacy as the Default Setting
•Privacy Embedded into Design
•Full Functionality — Positive-Sum, not Zero-Sum
•End-to-End Security — Full Lifecycle Protection
•Visibility and Transparency — Keep it Open
•Respect for User Privacy — Keep it User-centric

Extending beyond FIPs, PbD represents a significant “raising” of the bar in the area of privacy protection.
