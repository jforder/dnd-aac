Section 6

Emergence of Privacy by Design (PbD)

Privacy by Design (PbD) is a framework developed since the mid-1990s by Ontario's Information and Privacy Commissioner, Dr. Ann Cavoukian, in an effort to shift the conventional method of privacy protection from a reactive regulation and compliance model to a proactive preventative model. PbD originally focused on information technology, but grew to include organizational practices, and information eco-systems. In October 2010, International Privacy and Data Protection Commissioners unanimously recognized PbD as an “essential component of fundamental privacy protection” , and committed to promote the incorporation of its Privacy by Design Foundational Principles in privacy policy and legislation in their respective jurisdictions, and to encourage the adoption of PbD principles as part of an organization's default mode of operation.” Since then, PbD has figured prominently in regulatory oversight reform proposals in the US and EU. More than a concept, Privacy by Design is becoming a legal and regulatory requirement in major jurisdictions around the world.
